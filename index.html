The Temple of the 5 Elements 

is a meditation on love and the fulfilled hope of re/connection. After two years mostly apart, I wanted to create an idea that would bring friends, old and new, together to embrace, maybe kiss, and experience the joy of human connection that has been absent for too long and for too many. My intent is for this installation to inspire people to interact with it and with each other, which will be achieved since it will take two people standing on the disk to trigger th e effect. My hope is it reawakens a sense of love for each other. That love is elemental. That love is the light we need to guide us through the darkness we experience in our lives. And have fun in the process.

The Temple of the 5 Elements is officially returning to the playa in 2023!

To bring the Temple of the 5 Elements to the playa we need your support.

2022 was a challenging year for To5E and even the most veteran burners on our team learned new lessons on how the playa can undo even the best, tested plans. So we are upgrading nearly every system in 2023 for maximum uptime. New lighting, new stones, new power and data cabling, a whole new show control system, new generator and box, much more are all in progress with the goal to run every night from dusk until dawn.

Every donation made will be received with love, the thanks of the TO5E team and the thousands of people who will experience this project on the playa.

About To5E, LLC

To5E, LLC is a legal entity established to provide an ethical means of managing the donations for the project that will be transparent to all members. We do not expect to make a profit and any donations in excess of expected needs will be invested back into the project and/or cover other associated costs. Please note that our fundraising goal of $16,000 is not the total cost of project, it’s the minimum we need to make it happen.

The To5E Team

Matt Scott

Dr. Scott (Matt Scott) is a 18yr burner, burning since 2005. He is a TCO for Camp Monkey Business and has built much of the camp’s infrastructure including the Banana Beacon.

Dr. Scott has volunteered on art & media projects including Man-O-Morphosis, Mucaro, and “The Art of Burning Man.” In the pandemic years, he created “City of Dust” a 7-min 3DVR film for BMVR screened in Videogasam’s virtual camp. In his personal life, Dr. Scott makes IMAX nature docs and teaches IMAX filmmaking at USC.

Diana Bradbury

Diana is a 7 year Burning Man attendee and mixed media artist originally from Reno, Nevada, now living in Highland Park, Los Angeles. She was the camp administrator for Our House Camp for 5 years and was a crewmember for RVFMTV2, an art installation inside the man-base in 2012.

She is currently creating a brand of housewares and clothes using mostly upcycled items called @sadbradbury. You can follow her at @dirtydiana on instagram and twitter.
