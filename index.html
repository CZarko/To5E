Heading Back To The Playa With Big Upgrade For 2023

Temple of The 5 Elements - 2.0.23

The Temple of the 5 Elements 

is a meditation on love and the fulfilled hope of re/connection. After two years mostly apart, I wanted to create an idea that would bring friends, old and new, together to embrace, maybe kiss, and experience the joy of human connection that has been absent for too long and for too many. My intent is for this installation to inspire people to interact with it and with each other, which will be achieved since it will take two people standing on the disk to trigger the effect. My hope is it reawakens a sense of love for each other. That love is elemental. That love is the light we need to guide us through the darkness we experience in our lives. And have fun in the process.



Temple of The 5 Elements - is an art installation for Burning Man 2023, inspired by The Fifth Element, that will allow you to transform into the element of love.

Experience: On the playa, two or more participants will ascend to the top of a short altar to stand on an inset, clear plexiglass disk, triggering hidden pressure sensors. When activated, beams of light will shoot out from four surrounding pillars in colors representing Earth, Air, Water, and Fire onto the participants on the altar. A powerful beam of white light will illuminate them from below as they kiss or embrace, illuminating the surrounding area as they become the element of love until the lighting effect times out and fades to make space for other participants.

The To5E Team

Matt Scott

Dr. Scott (Matt Scott) is a 18yr burner, burning since 2005. He is a TCO for Camp Monkey Business and has built much of the camp’s infrastructure including the Banana Beacon.

Dr. Scott has volunteered on art & media projects including Man-O-Morphosis, Mucaro, and “The Art of Burning Man.” In the pandemic years, he created “City of Dust” a 7-min 3DVR film for BMVR screened in Videogasam’s virtual camp. In his personal life, Dr. Scott makes IMAX nature docs and teaches IMAX filmmaking at USC.
